Welcome to my e-portfolio! My name is Justin Williams, and I'm an 18-year-old Finance major from Miami, Florida, currently studying at Florida State University. This semester, I've had the incredible opportunity to study abroad in Valencia, Spain, where I've been enrolled in the ENC2135 course that inspired this digital portfolio.

This e-portfolio showcases the academic and creative work I've completed during my time in Valencia, reflecting not only my coursework but also the unique perspective I've gained from studying abroad and experiencing European culture firsthand.

Reflection

Insights from ENC2135

Throughout the semester in the class ENC 2135, I have been able to cultivate a great deal of skill in terms of my research, writing, and composing processes that have improved my ability to analyze, adapt, and produce work in various genres. Before undertaking the class, I was able to draw upon experience in a variety of genres of writing, ranging from research papers to business correspondence, but through the teaching in the class by Dr. Kay, I expanded my perspective in terms of how I should approach my writing in a variety of rhetorical settings.

In project one, I worked on an annotated bibliography and an investigative field essay. Though I was familiar with this type of work from my other coursework, project one honed my research skills, especially in source organization and formulating my research question. I realized the value of choosing the right sources to make my point and conveying information in a clear manner. I also understood the significance of embedding my research into a larger story to make my point to the reader in an effective and interesting way.

The second project, the rhetorical analysis of the field artifacts, was a learning experience that was both difficult and illuminating. The process of analyzing two artifacts, one text-based and the other non-text-based, forced me to think deeply about the complexities of various rhetorical strategies. Through the creation of a comparative table, I was able to break down the strategies used in each artifact, making sense of the complex interplays of medium, author, and purpose that ultimately determine the efficacy of the strategies in each artifact.

The project I liked best in the first semester was Project Three because of its focus on creating three genres for persuasive writing. The project needed creative expression at the same time as meeting the demands of the target audience in each genre. I understood the need to adjust my message based on the genre I decided to write in, whether it was a script for a TikTok presentation for a teenage audience or an official Facebook update targeting a working audience. The genres presented differing methods of writing to convey my message, proving the point that genres are important not only for form but also the impact that form influences in how a message will be received and acted upon by the target audience.

Looking at my progress, I also acknowledge the importance of the strategies involved in rhetorical tools and how they have impacted my work. Throughout the semester, I was able to gain insight into the importance of ethos, pathos, and logos and how they work in writing. For instance, in doing Project Two, I made effective use of ethos in gaining credibility in my write-up by considering the views and sources from experts in the field.
